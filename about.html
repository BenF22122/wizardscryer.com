About Wizard of the Scrying Tower

In the quiet halls of the Scrying Tower, where circuits hum like arcane whispers and screens glow like enchanted runes, a single scroll rests upon a stone pedestal.

Its parchment is warm to the touch, as though the magic within it still stirs. Upon its surface is inscribed the tale of a Wizard born not of myth, but of code.

A guardian forged to illuminate the hidden workings of machines and guide travellers through the labyrinth of Windows.

The Origin of the Wizard

Long before the Wizard took form, the lands of Windows were troubled. Errors lurked like mischievous sprites, performance waned under unseen curses, and travellers wandered lost among cryptic messages and unfriendly tools. Many sought answers, yet the tomes they found spoke only in riddles and cold diagnostics.

Thus, the Scrying Tower stirred. From circuits and spellcraft, from engineering and enchantment, it forged a new kind of mage one who could translate the arcane into the understandable, reveal truths with clarity and stand beside novices and masters alike. The Wizard was created not to confuse, but to enlighten. Not to overwhelm, but to empower.

The Wizard’s Philosophy

The Scrying Tower teaches that magic is not meant to intimidate, but to illuminate. The Wizard holds fast to this creed:

Knowledge should be shared, not hoarded. Diagnostics should enlighten, not confuse. Power should be accessible, not hidden behind arcane barriers. And above all, the traveller’s privacy is sacred. No spell shall ever betray it.

The Wizard of the Scrying Tower is 100% local. No spell shall ever send information outside the user’s machine.

The Creator

Though the Wizard is a creature of code and enchantment, the Tower itself is tended by a single caretaker, Ben Fletcher. An engineer of the modern age, he has walked the winding paths of networks, systems, and hardware, mastering the arts that bind machines together.

From his craft and curiosity, the Wizard was born. Not as a product, but as a companion — a helpful spirit forged to make technology feel less like a beast and more like an ally. The Tower grows because he tends it, and the Wizard learns because he teaches it.
